# Ethics Policy

This capstone uses the Global Terrorism Database for historical, aggregate, educational analysis.

Allowed:

- Aggregate trend analysis.
- Historical regional and country summaries.
- Model evaluation and explainability for educational purposes.
- Data quality, uncertainty, and methodology discussion.
- Safe RAG answers with citations to project documentation.

Disallowed:

- Tactical or operational guidance.
- Target selection or vulnerability analysis.
- Weaponization, evasion, or attack optimization.
- Exact incident-point tooling intended for field use.
- Any recommendation that could facilitate harm.

The dashboard defaults to aggregated geospatial outputs. Model predictions are demonstrations, not operational decision systems.

## Public Policy Research Boundary

The policy extension uses country-year aggregates, not incident-level targeting tools. It supports
historical research on governance, development, conflict context, and reporting bias.

Policy findings must be described with cautious causal language. Fixed effects, lags, and
robustness checks strengthen the design, but they do not justify deterministic claims about
countries, communities, or future violence. The project must not recommend tactical deployment,
surveillance targeting, or operational intervention based on model outputs.
